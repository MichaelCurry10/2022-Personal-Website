About me

Student at Queen's University

I’ve always loved to build things. It all started when I was five, driving remote control cars to their limit and deconstructing them minutes after they broke down on me. Then, in grades 9 and 10, I was introduced to woodworking, welding, and auto mechanics. I was able to build my high school’s fastest-ever CO2 car, which holds the record to this day. Those interests pivoted when I took my first computer engineering course in grade 11, where I became fascinated with circuit boards, 3D modelling with Fusion 360, and robotics. In 2021, I found myself in the Queen’s Commerce Program at the Smith School of Business and have since truly found a passion for bringing together the world of Technology and Business.

Outside of my studies, I spend time at the gym, playing volleyball, keeping up with the Sneaker and Watch markets, programming, and exploring entrepreneurial ventures.

I am the youngest of four kids in my family and get along with all of my siblings. I live in a rural part of Kanata and have two Golden Retrievers. I love spending time at my cottage and enjoy canoe trips with my family when we can all get together to go on one. I am currently breaking all kinds of records on Duolingo as I learn Spanish for my upcoming semester at Esade in Spain!
